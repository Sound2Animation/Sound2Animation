About

Sound2Motion simulates realistic rigid body physics using GPU-accelerated XPBD solver and synthesizes impact sounds from real recorded audio samples from the RealImpact dataset.

Our system combines material-aware physics parameters with impulse-based sound synthesis to create physically plausible animations with synchronized audio.

Newton Physics

GPU-accelerated XPBD solver via NVIDIA Warp

Material-Aware

Ceramic, iron, wood, plastic, glass properties

Sound Synthesis

Impulse-driven synthesis from RealImpact recordings

ML Integration

Diffusion model for audio-conditioned trajectories

Technical Details

Physics Simulation

  • XPBD solver with 10 iterations
  • 120 FPS with 12 substeps
  • Material-specific density and restitution
  • Contact-aware collision detection

Sound Synthesis

  • Impulse detection (J = m × Δv)
  • Volume scaling based on impulse magnitude
  • Closest hit point selection from RealImpact
  • Angle-based audio sample selection

ML Pipeline

  • Diffusion model for trajectory generation
  • Audio conditioning via Mel spectrogram CNN
  • MDM-style trajectory encoding (13D)
  • Contact-aware loss weighting

Rendering

  • Blender 4.x (EEVEE/Cycles)
  • 1280×720 @ 60 FPS
  • Z-up coordinate system
  • ffmpeg audio/video combining
